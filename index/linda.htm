linda

 

Two men sit in the center of a large room with walls that were once white but now are stained with filth and brown.  The men, like the wall, are dirty and browned.  One is laughing loudly and not to himself and not to the other.  The other sits, still and stewing.  Suddenly, the man is silent while the other spits into the corner.  He bends and grabs his boots, laces them, and ties them.  He stands to retrieve his coat.

 

Where’re you going.

Work.

When’ll you be back.

I reckon I’ll be back when its done.

Ok.

 

He dons his coat and twists the handle of the door, pulls it open and steps out of the room into the outside and pulls the door closed again.  Moments later he returns to the quieted laughter of the other man.

 

Work?

Not today.

Not any day.

When I feel the impulse.

What’re you going to eat.

Take off your boots.  You’re getting mud and shit everywhere.

It seems to fit.

 

The man begins to laugh.

 

You’re drunk

As a skunk.

Reckless son of a bitch.

I’ll work when I’m dead.

Have another drink.

I believe I will.

 

The man crosses to retrieve the drink.  The other man stretches to untie his boots.  A can explodes where his head used to be.

 

 

Son of a bitch.

Slipped.  I aimed for your skull.

I’ll be the end of you.

Not today.

Sleep with one eye open.

Always do.  Beer?

Yes.

Catch.

Good throw.

Been working on it.

Yes.

 

The men cross to the middle of the room and sit.  One in brown the other in brown work clothes and soiled socks.  They stare at the wall but it does nothing.  They drink their beer in silence and the man spits at the corner.  The other man does so in a like manner.  He sips his beer.  Again.  He swallows, more gulps.  Again, he spits.  The other does the same.  He moves to scratch his nose but instead picks his ear.  The other pulls wax from his nose and flicks it at the corner.  He stands.

 

Where’re you going?

Drink.  Want one?

I don’t drink.

Liar.

Where’d you go today. 

Work.

Liar.  Saw you get your pick up stuck today.

So.

You’d be late for work.

Nope.  Arrived just in time.

Where’s my drink?

Catch.

 

He does and the man crosses back and sits beside the other.  They crack their drink and stare at the wall which does not react.  They pour their drink down their gullets.  One man in brown the other in pants and long shirt both brown with stains or age and both.  His socks stiff and hard, almost yellow.

 

What’re you watching.

Nothing.  Nothings on.

Change it.

Its broken.

Shit.

What’ll we do.

Get a new one.

What do you do everyday?

Work.

Bull shit.

Bull true.

Prove it.

Come in with me tomorrow.

Where?

Work.

What should I wear?

Clothes.

Have any boots?

Yeah, wear my extras.

I’ve not been to work in a while.

Go tomorrow.

Don’t know what I’m worried about, you don’t go to work.

Guess we’ll find out.

Yep.

 

The man finish their drink and set it aside.  They stretch out and lie down.

 

Night.

See you tomorrow.

Work.

Yep.

 

The men slept.  He slept in contentment and full of satisfaction with no room for guilt or unease though some crept up anyway unbidden.  He tossed and turned the night though his body lie still.  The other’s full of nightmares and drowning, spasms in fits and hacking, reeking of swill and other things vile.  In wild jerks he hit the other man and he awoke.  Though neither did remember next day.

 

What time is it.

Time to get up.

Work?

Yep.

Ok.  Boots?

Over there.

 

The men don their boots, one on socks dry now from sweat and other, the other on feet bare and soft.  He puts on a hat and walks out.  He follows, head naked as his toes.

 

Its gonna rain.

Shit.

 

He walks out the door and closes it.  Nothing.  Seconds later the door reopens and he enters followed by the other man.  They look tired and haggard.  The man crosses to the center of the room and sits.  The man removes his boots and crosses and grabs a beer.

 

            Want one?

            No.

 

A spider hangs by a thread in front of the man who sits on the floor.  He lights a cigarette and blows smoke rings around and through the spider.  It does not react and the smoke rings circle around into the man’s face.  He looks at the other man.  He grins.

 

            Can I have a smoke?

            No.  My last one.

            Let me have a drag at least.

            Tell me what you’re smiling at.

            Nothing.  A joke.  It isn’t funny.

            An unfunny joke which you were smiling at.  Why’d you smile.

            Don’t recollect.

            Have a seat.

            Why didn’t you take your boots off.

            Don’t recollect.

 

A pause which is filled with smoke, chagrin, and levitating spiders interrupted by

 

            Just remember to take them off next time.

            Ok.

           

Another pause filled by the ticking of a small brown watch on the left wrist of the man who smokes his cigarette.  The other man crosses and sits next to the man.  The other man cracks his beer and drinks.  The man smokes his cigarette and exhales away from the other man and the spider which hangs still and up his web.  The man watches it go as smoke drifts up between his fingertips and the other man watches him.  He drags and the embers alight.  He sips and the foam sizzles.

 

            That never happened before.

            No.  Didn’t think so.

            That damn rain.

            Yep.  Muddy.

            Thought I could make it through.

            Good thing you didn’t.

            Coulda made it.

            Maybe.

            Never happened before.

 

Spider web like silk hangs and flies buzz around their faces avoiding traps and snares and cotton flares but never stills.  The man stabs his cigarette out and throws the butt in the corner.  The other man stands and crosses to the corner and picks up the butt and crosses back and sits.

 

            Gotta light.

 

He hands him a match and he lights the end of the cigarette.

 

            Good smoke.

            Yeah.

            You roll it.

            Yep.

            Good.

            Yes.

 

Vultures circle high above their wings spread and soaring.

 

            You going to work tomorrow.

            Nope.  One days enough.

            Didn’t enjoy yourself then.

            No.  Got it better here.

            What’re you going to eat.

            My fucking feet.

 

He throws the butt in the corner still lit along with the drink unfinished.  It spills on the butt and it sizzles.  He lies down.

 

            Damn raptors are circling us again.

            I’ll stay awake.

            You sure?

            Yes.

            You can wake me if you want to get some sleep.

            No.  You got work.

            Ok. 

 

The spider has floated away and the flies have dissipated leaving the men and vultures.  He watches them circle and the other man closes his eyes and rolls on his side facing him.  His breathing deepens and slows.  The man removes a cigarette and lights it with a match.  He throws it in the corner.  He smokes his cigarette and it follows the match.  Soon after the vultures circles widen to elsewhere and the other man sits up.  He is awake and rubs his eyes and gains his bearings.  He looks up.

 

            I had the strangest dreams.

            I haven’t slept.

            You’re still wearing your boots.

            I haven’t taken them off yet.

            Where’re the raptors?

            They left.  Last to leave.

            You been up all night.

            I didn’t sleep.

            You could’ve woke me up.

            You got work.

            Must be tired.

            Been smoking all night.

            I can only imagine your state of mind.

            Weary and blurred.

            Not too unlike mine.

            What were your dreams.

            I can’t remember.

            Too bad.

            There were shapes and feelings like suffocating or drowning or claustrophobia

            and the shapes are falling all over each other becoming the same like one and

            everything is brown and it smells and everything is the same and moving and I’m

            in the middle and I can’t move.

            I thought you couldn’t remember.

            I can’t.

            Sounds like a real nightmare.

            I suspect it was.  Work?

            Not today.

            Not any day.

            Still don’t know where you go.

            Work.

            I don’t believe you for a single goddamned second.

            Where do you think you were all day?

            Some goddamned place.

 

The man crosses to put on his boots and don his coat.  There is something caked onto the shoelaces and the toes haven’t yet dried.  His socks soak through instantly.  They are stained brown.

           

            Take off your damn boots.  You’re tracking shit everywhere.

 

He opens the door and walks out.  It is raining.  The other man lights a cigarette and falls asleep.  A bee flies silently about his head and lands on his nose and crawls inside.  He wrinkles his nose and scratches his ear.  There is a dull sound, a muffled chainsaw, sliding gravel, snapping twigs, the bee flies out and the man opens glazed eyes and the bee lands on his left eye and rests on his pupil.  He sleeps until his cigarette has burned all the way down and burns his fingertips.

 

            Ouch.

 

He blinks and the bee flies away.  He stands and crosses to get a drink and stops halfway.  He turns to look at the wall.  He stares and the wall stares back.

 

            What’re you looking at, you still, greasy fucker.

 

He is still and the wall is still and he flips it off and gets a beer and sits down in the middle of the room.  The beer sweats and the sweat stains his hand and he doesn’t yet open it but stares at the wall and the wall stares back and he stands and curses the wall and damns it and hurls his unopened beer and kicks and stomps and screams and pulls his hair out and finally sits down and falls asleep again and soon the door opens and the other man walks in and takes his boots off and takes the boots off the other man and gets a beer and sits next to the man and he drinks his beer and throws his empty into the corner and falls asleep.  A line of ants crosses in front of the dozing men leading one direction and not back.  Soon the men sit up and blink back sleep from their eyes. 

 

            Work?

            Not today.  Weekend.

            Going out?

            Yep.

            Where?

            Somewhere else.

            Why can’t you tell me.

            I can, I don’t want to.

            You’re never around anymore.

            I got other places to be.

            Just go.  Don’t let the door hit you on the ass.

 

He dons his boots, ties them, salutes the other man sitting on the floor smoking a cigarette, opens the door and leaves.

 

            Make like a damn leaf.

 

Alone he stands and walks and gets a drink and sits, cracks his beer and drinks.  And he drinks until the raptors are back and cobwebs float like silk and a bee flies to pollinate and even parasites come out.  The parasites crawl into his ears and he scratches at them with his nails at his arms and neck but they come anyway.  Against his will he is overcome and he succumbs, lying back in surrender and disgust and without hope and the parasites overcame his body entire, enveloped and entombed, mummified by parasites and instead of wire, parasites pierce holes and empty orifices and he is suffocating and drowning in them awaiting an arrival or return but finding none and suffering quietly while the wall watches in pacification.

 

Treachery abounds.  Me entwined.  I’m caught up in this, this is where I belong, where I have it made, where I have made it, I ain’t going nowheres where I’m not welcome, anywhere’s where I feel most comfortable.

 

A pit opens slowly in the middle of the room edging slowly towards the man who drinks.  It is black and deep with no apparent bottom and its edges are jagged and steep.

 

It is coming, I can feel it in my bones, where my marrow lies, something ominous and dangerous and full of teeth, or perhaps one day it has finally come.  Oh, wretched hole swallow me whole.

 

The door opens with a slam and the hole rescinds with the abruptness of the sound and Linda walks in wearing the horns of a goat.

 

Demon!  Where is the hole?

 

Don’t you throw that fuckin beer at me, you sum bitch!  Unless theres sumfin in it.  I ain’t no garbage can.

 

What are you.

 

She speaks garbled and full of spit and he can’t understand her.

 

I’m Linda.  I’m your worst fuckin nightmare.

 

Linda’s mouth moves and a sudden series of sharp brays come out and she begins to run on her arms and legs and buck and kick and bray and the man watches.

 

What do you want?  Who sent you?

 

I was sent by no thing I apparitioned.  I can’t leave neither I’m here to stay s’long s’want.

 

She sings the last words in chanting rhythms and the man is quietly epileptic in fear.

 

I can’t understand you.  You beast, be gone, away, be gone!  Where have all the raptors gone?

 

Ain’t no raptors round about these parts just me.  And I don’t do no praying neither.  I’m here to ill bode and hyperventilate I’m here to invite lust and sin and grave mistakes.  You’ll see.  Everythings about to come crashing down crystal clear as mud.

 

The spiders.  The spiders are gone.  Where are the parasites?

 

There you go thats more my speed yore talking my language now.  Parasites are the rage everybodys doin it.  This heres a paradise for parasites a regular bloodsuckers beach.  Jump in the waters fine warm tepid just right as shed say.  Who are you to decline in what age and time would you be permitted to say no. 

 

She begins to buck and bray again as he shields his eyes and averts his ears.  His mouth is locked open by his protruding tongue and his feet spasm in fits.  The door opens and the other man walks through and removes his boots and crosses and sits next to the man who is trapped in throes.  

 

Gotta smoke?

Smoke?  Don’t you see her?  She’s right there, don’t move!  Don’t draw her attention to us.

There’s no one else.  What’s your state of mind?

Maddening.  Is she not?

She is not.  Linda is coming by later.

What’s Linda?

She is a succubus.

what?

The girl I am courting.  She is coming by later.

What’s her name?

Her name is Linda.

Linda.

Yes.  Gotta smoke?

No.

 

He stands and crosses to the door and back again and back to the door.  He is pacing.  At the door he stops.

 

Where is it you go during the days?  Why can’t you tell me?  Why won’t you tell me?  I sit here all day and wonder and have a drink and wait for something to come but it never does until at last insects and vultures abound and holes creep up until another day comes Linda.  She is the end of all things and terrible.  The earth opens up.

You oughta take yore boots off fore Linda comes.

Why are you talking like that?

Like wut?

When is she coming over?  Linda.

Not sure didn’t say.  Only later.

This is ill boding.

She ain’t that bad you’ll like her.  That’s what she said to tell you. 

Tell me what.

You’ll like her.  Linda told me to tell you you’d like her.

 

There is a single knock at the door.  The men turn and look simultaneously, one grinning, the other groaning all but audibly.

 

Linda.

 

The door opens slowly, revolving in on itself, and a shadow intrudes itself into the room shielding all other light.  A shape expels itself from the shadow and there it presents.

 

Linda!

 

And who is this?

 

Hello.

 

You know each other.

 

                                                                                                No.

 

Good.  This is Linda.

 

Neither approach the other and neither move to welcome.  The other man crosses for a drink and grass grew and paint chipped and two faced each other and neither did blink.

 

Either of you want a drink?

 

I’ll have one.

 

She abruptly crosses to the other man brushing past the man her hair parting such and the man quickly sits.

 

None for me.

 

Must have had a big breakfast.

 

What have you done with your life?

 

Nothing.  Not a single goddamned thing.

 

You must do something.  Everybody has to do something.  How do you earn a living?

 

I don’t earn anything.

 

He has a job and prospects and friends.

 

She holds her drink in a glass in her right hand while he sits on the floor and the other man observes.  Somewhere else everything else begins to fall apart as well.

 

I don’t have anything.

 

Not even property or a semblance of a plan?

 

Not even a shitter.  I piss in the corner.

 

I was going to clean up before you got here, I must have forgot, I was so excited to see you, its been so long, and now you’re here and I’m just not really sure what else there is to do.

 

Get me another drink.

 

Me too.

 

Two drinks.  Don’t clam up on my account, offer more.

 

You’ve been here before.

 

Never.  This is my first time pardner.

 

I’d rather you not call me that.

 

He sits staring up at Linda as she postures her glass and the other man slowly pours.  It is filled and she drinks and steps above the man sitting on the floor.  The other man pours the rest of the beer onto the floor and stares at the spot where Linda’s glass had been.  It empties and his eyes follow.  The other man’s eyes are upon Linda’s own.

 

What else should I call you.

 

Don’t call me anything..

 

As things fall apart the man hands the man sitting on the floor a drink and crosses to the corner.  He cracks his beer and reaches down and grabs a cigarette butt from the floor.  He holds them and stares.

 

You should call me what I am.  Nothing.  I hardly exist.  I’m not even an amoeba.  I must be sleeping.

 

Disappear.

 

The other man crosses to the door, dons his boots and coat and leaves.

 

Have a drink.

 

I’m one too many already.

 

I’ve been here before.

 

I know.

 

I know you.

 

Yes.

 

I know what you’re thinking.

 

I am what I am.

 

You always were.  I know I was there.  I can see your eyes and I could then too.

 

Flies buzz and the parasites come out.  They have nowhere to go.

 

You know what I am.

 

Yes.

 

The vultures are above and worms sprout from the ground and leave trails of slime behind them.

 

Do you know why I’m here.

 

Yes.

 

Spiders with wings and teeth poach in the corner snacking on remnants.  Silky eggs roll across the floor and stick to the closest wall.  They break open and more pour out. 

 

You have no pride.

 

I pissed myself.

 

You’ve pissed your last.

 

I waited for the hole to eat me.  But now I’m surrounded by insects and parasites every which way and I don’t have anywhere to go but home but I have no home.  This used to be my home.

 

Snakes grow from Linda’s hair and flies steal into her pores and spiders hide in her eyes with their silky webs.

 

I woke up one day and it was gone and I looked around and didn’t recognize anything and felt parasites drinking my blood and feasting on my bones and marrow and my life was stolen before me and I opened my eyes and couldn’t see anything so I sat here and started drinking to poison those that would poison me.  I expected to be surrounded by their bodies.

 

Her skin crawls and her hair slithers and Linda waits patiently for the conclusion and the world is quiet.

 

Removed or destroyed as though nothing was ever there.  I did not understand so I stopped sleeping so I wouldn’t miss it but I did anyway because I had to cover my eyes to keep from seeing and my eyes burned from blinking back tears and I grew very angry at the carnage around me and nightmares came and went and came again and I grew angrier at my inabilities and I grew worse and violent. 

 

He closes his eyes and Linda swarms and fills the holes of the man with creatures and the man screams with buzzing insects into his open mouth.  Into his chest and arms and stomach and legs and behind his eyes their wings and teeth glimmer and glint and he is blinded.  His tongue is swallowed and his teeth turn to dust and he chokes and with each cough more winged beasts fill his lungs and he gags on his breath and in a hacking fit his appendages twitch and spasm and as suddenly is still.  There is a single knock at the door.
